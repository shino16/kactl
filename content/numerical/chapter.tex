\section{Newton's method for polynomials}
    To solve {\small $F(Q) \equiv 0 \pmod{x^D}$},\\
    let {\small $Q_{2k} \gets \left(Q_k - \frac{F(Q_k)}{F'(Q_k)}\right) \pmod {x^{2k}}$}.

    For polynomial $A$:\\
    $\frac{1}{A}$: $Q_1 \gets \frac{1}{a_0}$, $Q_{2k} \gets Q_k(2 - AQ_k)$\\
    $(\log A)' = \frac{A'}{A}$\\
    $\exp A$, $a_0 = 0$: $Q_1 \gets 0$, $Q_{2k} \gets Q_k(1 + A - \log Q_k)$\\
    $A^k = \exp (k \log A)$
